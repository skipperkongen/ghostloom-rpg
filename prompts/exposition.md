In storytelling (novels, films, plays, games), **exposition** provides the essential background information the audience needs to understand the story. The important elements are:

### Core Elements of Exposition

1. **Setting**

   * Time (historical period, season, time of day)
   * Place (country, city, specific locations)
   * World rules (realistic, fantastical, sci-fi, social norms)

2. **Main Characters**

   * Protagonist(s)
   * Key supporting characters
   * Basic relationships between characters

3. **Status Quo**

   * What “normal life” looks like before the main conflict
   * The character’s routine, role, or position in the world

4. **Backstory**

   * Past events that shape characters or the current situation
   * History of the world, family, or conflict (only what’s relevant)

5. **Central Conflict (Seed)**

   * The problem, tension, or imbalance that will drive the story
   * Often hinted at rather than fully developed

6. **Stakes**

   * What can be gained or lost
   * Why the conflict matters to the characters and the world

7. **Tone and Genre Signals**

   * Emotional mood (dark, comedic, hopeful, tense)
   * Genre expectations (fantasy, crime, romance, sci-fi)

8. **Point of View**

   * Who is telling or experiencing the story
   * First person, third person, omniscient, etc.

9. **Theme (Implied)**

   * Early hints at the story’s deeper ideas (power, freedom, family, justice)
   * Usually suggested, not stated

### Optional / Advanced Elements (Used When Needed)

10. **Inciting Context**

    * Conditions that make the inciting incident possible
    * Political, social, or technological setup

11. **Rules of Conflict**

    * What is allowed or forbidden in this world
    * Limits on power, magic, law, or technology

12. **Foreshadowing**

    * Subtle clues about future events or outcomes

---

**Good exposition** is:

* Relevant (only what the audience needs)
* Integrated (shown through action, dialogue, or environment)
* Incomplete (leaves questions unanswered)
